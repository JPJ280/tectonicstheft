"All weather and climate result from three fundamental entities: motion (both horizontal and vertical), energy, and moisture. Without any of these three, weather and climate do not exist. Any attempt to simulate the behavior of the atmosphere dynamically must include a representation of the fundamental equations that govern motion, energy, and moisture in the atmosphere. Seven equations accomplish this task. " - Rohli and Vega, "Climatology" Ch.13
`vecu` wind velocity
`rho` density
`p` pressure
`T` temperature
`m` specific humidity
`m = r/(r+1)` or `m = (e epsilon)/p`
`P` Precipitation
`P` Evaporation
`f` Coriolis force
`f = 2 Omega sin varphi`
`vecF` additional forcing
`C_p` constant pressure specific heat capacity
`R_d` dry air constant `(287 J kg^-1 K^-1)`
`R` universal gas constant `(8.314 J mol^-1 K^-1)`
`r` mixing ratio
`e` vapor pressure
`e = e_0 exp( (L M) / R (1/T_f - 1/T_d ) )`
`e_0` vapor pressure when `T_d = T_f` `(611 Pa)`
`T_f` Temperature at freezing
`T_d` Temperature at dew point
`T_v` Virtual temperature, temperature of dry air were its pressure and temperature equaled that of sampled air
`T_v = ((1+r/epsilon))/(1+r) T`
`L` latent heat of condensation `(2.5008e6 J kg^-1)` or sublimation `(2.8345e6 J kg^-1)` if over ice surface
Equation of State:
`P/rho = R_d T_v`
Mass Conservation Equation:
`(drho)/dt = -u(drho)/dx -v(drho)/dy - w(drho)/dz -rho(du)/dx -rho(dv)/dy - rho(dw)/dz` `(drho)/dt = -vecu * nabla rho + rho nabla * vecu`
Moisture Conservation Equation:
`(dm)/dt = -u(dm)/dx -v(dm)/dy - w(dm)/dz + P - E` `(dm)/dt = -vecu * nabla m + P - E`
Thermodynamic Energy Equation:
`(dT)/dt = -u(dT)/dx -v(dT)/dy - w(dT)/dz + 1/C_p (dQ)/dt + 1/(rho C_p) (dp)/dt` `(dT)/dt = -vecu * nabla T + 1/C_p (dQ)/dt + 1/(rho C_p) (dp)/dt`
Navier-Stokes Equations:
`(du)/dt = -u(du)/dx -v(du)/dy - w(du)/dz - 1/rho (dp)/dx + F_x + fv`
`(dv)/dt = -u(dv)/dx -v(dv)/dy - w(dv)/dz - 1/rho (dp)/dy + F_y - fu`
`(dw)/dt = -u(dw)/dx -v(dw)/dy - w(dw)/dz - 1/rho g + F_z`

`(dvecu)/dt = -vecu * nabla vecu - 1/rho nabla p + vecF + fv`
